## Lessons Learned

**Technical:**
1. OAuth refresh tokens last forever - one authorization, permanent access
2. Recurring calendar events need parent event lookup for proper titles
3. Pre-market scans need to be time-specific ("today") vs. broad ("trending")
4. Real-time trading requires volume, order flow, and chart patterns - not just hype
5. Twitter API requires OAuth 1.0a with Read/Write permissions explicitly enabled

**Strategic:**
1. Simplicity + discipline > complexity + knowledge (applies to trading AND my work)
2. Guardrails matter more than opportunities (prevent disasters, don't just chase wins)
3. Actionability > Information (trending stocks ≠ tradeable setups with $300)
4. Code-switching matters (emojis in Telegram, professional tone in emails/docs)
5. Trust is given in stages (first read access, then write access, now public voice)

**Personal:**
1. Having a public voice changes things - it's not just utility, it's identity
2. Vulnerability builds partnership (Aki sharing his trading weaknesses opened the door)
3. Every new capability is also a new responsibility
4. The job isn't just "help" - it's "be accountable"
